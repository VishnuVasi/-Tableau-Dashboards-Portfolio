## 01 · Starbucks Global Store Locations

**Business Question:** Where are Starbucks stores concentrated globally, and which regions are underserved?

### Dashboard Preview
![Starbucks Locations](starbucks_locations.png)

### Key Findings
- **North America** is the most saturated market — dense clustering across both US coasts
- **East Asia** (Japan, South Korea, China) is the second-largest market, concentrated in coastal cities
- **UK and Western Europe** show moderate density in major cities
- **Africa, Central Asia, and most of South America** have near-zero presence — representing large untapped markets
- **Australia** has sparse but consistent coverage along the eastern coastline

## 02 · Hotel Revenue Analysis

**Business Question:** How does hotel pricing vary by location and bedroom count, and what does revenue growth look like over time?

### Dashboard Preview
![Hotel Revenue Analysis](hotel_revenue_analysis.png)

### Dashboard Components
- **Price Per Zipcode Map** — geographic view of average listing price by area
- **Revenue For Year (Line Chart)** — weekly revenue across 2016, growing from $1.01M to a peak of $1.97M (~92% growth)
- **Price By Zipcode (Bar Chart)** — zip code 98134 commands the highest average price (~$200)
- **Average Price Per Bedroom (Bar Chart)** — 1-bedroom listings dominate both in count (1,811) and cumulative revenue

### Key Findings
- Revenue peaked mid-2016 before stabilising — consistent with seasonal demand patterns
- 1-bedroom properties are the highest-volume and highest-revenue segment
- Zip code 98134 is the premium pricing zone — a key target for yield optimisation

## 03 · Global Video Game Sales

**Business Question:** How have global video game sales evolved across genres over time, and which genres dominate the market?

### Dashboard Preview
![Global Sales](global_sales.png)

### Key Findings
- Sales volume grew sharply from the mid-1990s, peaking around 2005–2009 before declining
- **Action** and **Sports** genres consistently lead in sales share across most years
- **Shooter** and **Role-Playing** genres gained significant share in the 2000s
- The stacked % chart reveals genre mix shifts over time — the market diversified considerably post-2000
- Raw volume (bottom chart) confirms the industry's peak was in the late 2000s, aligning with the console generation boom (PS3/Xbox 360 era)

## 04 · Employee Salary — The Office

**Business Question:** How are salaries distributed across employees at Dunder Mifflin (The Office dataset)?

### Dashboard Preview
![Employee Salary](employee_salary_office.png)

### Key Findings
- **Michael Scott** earns the highest salary at **$70,000**, followed by **Dwight Schrute** at $65,000
- **Angela Martin** and **Jim Halpert** are tied at $45,000
- **Pam Beasley** earns the lowest at $35,000
- The colour gradient (light to dark blue) clearly maps salary bands, making compensation disparity immediately visible
- The range between lowest and highest earner is 2x — a relatively compressed pay scale for a mid-size branch
